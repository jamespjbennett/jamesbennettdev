Story


Life before code

I was brought up a music man, studying it to degree level and spending a few years songwriting and gigging (listen here if you're interested..). After realising that perhaps an employable career was a good idea - I discovered my interest and aptitude for coding around 2013-14 - before taking the plunge and enrolling on General Assembly's Web Development course in early 2015.

Life learning code

Going back to school turned out to be a really good idea - I loved the course and everything seemed to click. There was a heavy emphasis on Ruby/Ruby on Rails (which I have stuck with and have a lot of love for) - whilst also getting a full introduction to javascript/PostgreSQL/HTML/CSS and various other web technologies.

I was selected to assist the teaching on the preceeding course, which was a fantastic way of consolidating knowledge and passing it onto others. I then jumped straight into the real world and landed a job with Purr Digital - a boutique development agency in London.

Professional Code life

At Purr I have moved from initially being the sole Rails Developer to managing a team of 3 - and eventually on to heading up the development team. Whilst here, I have had a chance to work on some fascinating technical challenges (see projects here). All of the projects have been worked on in an agile manner - delivering short sprints and advising the clients on next steps accordingly.

From a technical point of view, my team and I have been able to provide our own points of view on evolving our processes - many of which I have driven. This has included our establishing of an in-house QA system, making our code more test-driven, improving our implementation of appropriate design/refactoring patterns and query optomization. Whilst my side of the team are still 100% rails on the server side - we have introduced ReactJS into our arsenal so our Rails work can become more API-driven and lightweight.

Life at home

In my spare time I try to supplement my day job by improving my problem solving skills - and am currently fascinated by learning more about algorithms and how I can make them more efficient. Life isn't (quite) all about code - and I still like to produce music - as a side hustle I sell stock/library music for commercial purposes. I'm big into my football, although slightly depressed as to the state of my beloved Manchester United.
