JumpQ

The Project

JumpQ provide a food pre-ordering service, intially designed for and trialled at airports. We were briefed to build a mobile application, software to use on a POS system, and a backoffice admin system.

Tech

Ionic for the Android/IOS build, Ruby on Rails for the API/backend, PostgreSQL Database, AWS/elasticbeanstalk for hosting.Rspec for testing.

My Role

Simultaneously Project Leader and Tech Lead. Worked closely with and advised client to make the project a reality. Managed team of 4 rotating devs to bring the project to life. Built and tested the majority of the API and POS system, went to airport in Denmark to train staff how to use systems.

Runagood

The Project

Runagood provide an online business consultancy tool, with online tools to fully analyse your business finances and forecasting. We were initially briefed to build a one-page business finance questionnaire which used an algorithm to analyse you financial performance. The scope expanded to include more tools such as a business forecasting graph, business planning tools, and business improvement method tools - as well as an shop with which to buy products on a subscription basis

Tech

Ruby on Rails build throughout. Sass for the styling. Integration with Freeagent, Xero and Stripe APIs. Rspec/Capybara for testing.

My Role

Tech lead on the project throughout, moving onto more of Project Leader towards the latter phases of the project. Planned, built and tested the project, and was in weekly contact with the client to amend and developt project as part of an agile process.

Premier League Predictor

The Project

A personal project whereby you can simulate premier league football matches/seasons. Uses realistic game logic and player stats to anaylse probabilty and an interface to see results, tables and stats. Intention is to extend to allow users to pick teams and play against eachother.

Tech

Pure javascript. After being embedded in jQuery for so long - I wanted to achieve something on the front end with pure javascript. Adopted algorithm I found for generating fixtures, and created my own probability logic to determind the likely scores and scorers.
